False Identity
- Board Game Design

False Identity is a Board Game I created based on teamwork and deception, which can be tricky. My main objective with this game is to help players develop some soft skills like their ability to form relationships with others and their improv thinking skills.


This game was developed entirely from scratch and by hand, except for the cards made with procreate and then printed and cutted.


With this is mind, False Identity is a character based game where you will need to impersonate your character and everyone needs to interact with each other in various unpredictable scenarios.

01 Overview

•

False Identity

False Identity is a game about deception, strategy and improv thinking, all in a character based board game. It's a game for 3 to 10 players (recommended but can be played with less or more), ages 13 and up. Each player has a character, that they need to impersonate flawlessly, until the end of the game.

You might be a professional assassin, a hippnotherapist, a Japanese chef, a photographer… or, if you don't like any of the suggestions, you can even create your own character! You will navigate the board through your avatar, trying to pass challenges and prove that you are who you're pretending to be in order to win.

02 Assets

•

Board

For the base of my board I decided to go with a simple rectangular shape since it's one of the most popular formats and I figured it would be more harmonious when combined with the other assets. For that, I developed a grid that is rectangular on the sides and circular on the centre, around the cardholders. I wanted to give my board a visual sense of the everyday life of a person, resembling a common everyday place like a street, since, the game takes place in a "small society".

02 Assets

•

Spots

On top of the base there are the spots of the game. For this spots, I wanted something that was simple and would quickly communicate to the player the type if situation he would be in if he landed on that spot. For that, I used simplified shapes and symbols that are visually intuitive. I created all of them with the “identity icon” and their base to make the board visually harmonious and aesthetic.

02 Assets

•

Cardholders

For the cardholders I decided to maintain the same visuals of the base of the board, maintaining both the colour palette and transforming the spots' icons into actual pieces, that could hold the cards, in an efficient way so that people could take the cards out of the cardholders easily.

02 Assets

•

Cards

The cards are divided into various categories, differenciated by colour and symbology. In order to have a coherent design, I used the "identity sybol" of the spots, divided it in half and discreetly placed it, four times making a pattern, in the cover of each card. Some other cards still have the symbol incorporated in a different way.

02 Assets

•

Characters

The characters of these game were developed in procreated. They were inspired by a digital artist on Pinterest called Haku. They portrait the minimalistic and colouful design of the game, giving it more personality.

02 Assets

•

Pawns & Chips

For the pawns, I decided, once more, to use the “identity icon” in order to keep the aesthetic of the game flowing. Besides, the colours are the same as the category cards' ones so the game would have more colour and still be harmonious. For the chips, I decided to decorate them with the previously established pattern in order to visually attach the chips to the rest of the assets.

03 Game Rules

•

How to Play

For starters, each player's set consists of 5 Chips, 1 Character Card faced down and 4 Mission Cards, all of them chosen anonymously. You will be the only one aware of your identity in the beginning of the game. Regarding you Mission Cards, you will have to complete them and, each time you do, you get a chip. Only after completing all of your Mission Cards, will you be eligible to win. Roll the dice to determine the order in which the players will take their Character and Mission cards, randomly. The first person to take their cards will also be the first to play, after that person the game follows the normal clock order. The players should assign one of them to be in charge of the chips. This player will make sure everyone gets the chips they deserve throughout the game. But be careful with your choice since this person might try to take some not deserved chips for their own benefit and, in this game, small cheating moves like this are allowed! (As long as 1 round goes by without anyone noticing).

Each time someone completes a challenge from one of the solo categories (Watch Out and Finnish The Lyrics) successfully, they receive the number of chips listed on the card of the challenge they just completed. Besides, when a player completes a challenge, that same player is also allowed to ask, any other player they want, a question about their identity. If the player is sure about the opponents identity after the question was made, they can try to guess who the other player is. But careful, if you guess wrongfully, you will need to give one of your chips to the other player but, if you guess correctly the other player will give you one of their chips and turn their Character Card up so everyone can see their identity. Direct questions like “What is you identity?” are not allowed and the player to who the question was asked is not allowed to lie.

Throughout the game you will gain and lose chips, make and break alliances and some bad luck might ruin your game. The goal here is to be strategic so you can be the last player standing, that is, the winner. You lose the game if you lose all your chips. Every time a player loses, 1 extra spot of Prove It! Is added to the board on top of a different existing category spot, making the previous spot obsolete. When you lose the game you are not out of the fun! You will now play your part making life harder for those still playing. You will still be able to ask everything you want to the player that landed on the Prove It! spot. Besides, you can complicate the other players game by trading personality traits between them or take chips from the players without them noticing. If you can get through one round without them catching you, the changes you made to their set will be permanent. One round has passed when the player that was rolling the die when you made the change is playing again after everyone else got their turn. If you catch someone cheating and can prove to everyone they were actually cheating, that person is obligated to give you 1 chip. If you accuse someone but cannot prove it, you must give 1 chip to the person you accused. This does not apply to players that have already lost.
