Descarrilados
- Animated Short Story

Descarrilados was developed as a partnership project between 5 Global Design students and 3 Creative Tech students, from IADE. It was exhibited at Escola Secundária Marquês de Pombal, as part of a 25th of April celebration, marking the 50th year of the Portuguese Revolution.


The story focuses on showcasing the life of Portuguese people under the conservative regime of António de Oliveira Salazar, in a less serious tone and with some fantasy to it.

01 Theme

•

Revolution

Before the revolution of the 25th of April 1974, Portugal was under a conservative regime. Back then, a lot of the things we take for granted nowadays were forbidden and punished. As a group, we chose to showcase two of those forbidden behaviors of the time: (1) playing cards inside the train and (2) kissing in public.


02 Plot Overview

•

Descarrilados

The story unfolds on a cold night of November, in Lisbon, 1950. The streets are quiet, there are only a few people still outside, and who knows how many are Salazar's spies. It seems paranoid, but the truth is, if you cross the line, they always have a way to find out. But this night felt different; two men were determined to fight the regime. This night, they found out how dangerous a simple game of cards can be, how a conversation can influence and change the minds of people, and why this conservative leader fought so hard to prevent it.

04 Process

•

Character Design

This animation features a total of 6 different characters, each of them carefully thougth out. One main characteristic all of them share, is the fact that they have no face, in order to represent not just their actual persona, but a group of people, allowing the audience to relate to the plot easier. The King, the Queen and the 2 men on the train, for instance, represent the Portuguese people and the desire for freedom, while the Jack and the PIDE police represent the regime.

In the final render animation the main characters (King of Spades, Queen of Hearts and Jack of Clubs) had shaders, added in the shading process as a material, in order to give a 2D cartoonish effect to the animation.

04 Process

•

Environment Design

The setting of this short story is divided into 3 different environments. Firstly, there's the train station in which the action begins, which was carefully modeled to accurately represent Lisbon's Santa Apolónia station in the 1950's. One of the main elements of this environment is the train in which the characters get in. This train was also modeled to resemble “Foguete”, the 1953's train that traveled from Lisbon to Porto.

Secondly, we have the bar, in which the action in the card world begins. This bar was modeled with the purpose of representing an old cartoonish bar with a corolful theme and vintage feel. The third and final setting is the maze that connects these two worlds. Both the bar and the maze's final renders use shaders to mantain the cartoonish look of the card world, where the protagonists are the King, the Queen and the Jack.

04 Process

•

Title Design

Descarrilados is a portuguese word that means “derailed” or “off the rails”. We decided to use it as the name of our short story, not only because it's related to the main setting in which it takes place (the station and the train), but also because it works as a metaphor for the desire of the characters to break free and go “of the rails”.

In terms of typography, we used “Impact Regular”, converted the text into vectors and made changes in order for the lettering to match our story's visual aesthetic. For the letter "I", we decided to add a twist, by making the “body” of the letter resemble rails and the “head” of the letter into an actual head, resembling our characters. Besides, we decided to make the letters uneven, to visually represent the title. For the colour we chose red, not only because it's a high contrast and captivating colour, but also due to its symbolism since red was the colour of the revolution.

05 Marketing

•

Poster & Flyers

To promote our video, we developed a few visual elements to inform and capture the interest of the public. We designed and printed an A3 poster that would relate visually to the animation, containing elements from the actual video, creating a visual harmony. The main colours are red and dark blue. On the one hand, red is almost a mandatory colour to use, since it's the revolution colour. On the other hand, we also used dark blue since it's a dark colour that works well with the red, representing the negativity and uncertainty that was felt and lived during the 50s, which is when our story takes place.

To complement the poster, we also designed an A4 flyer that folds itself into an A5 piece of paper. The flyer is more complex and complete in terms of information than the poster since it contains a more detailed description and gives more context to our animation.

06 Merchandise

•

T-shirts

To create a more personal connection with the public, we decided to also develop some t-shirts. For the design, we implemented our typography, some character's concept art and some graphic elements related to the exhibition.
